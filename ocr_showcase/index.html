OCR tools on historical documents

Four OCR tools — olmOCR, Chandra 2, Gemini 3.5 Flash, and Infinity Parser 2 — compared across historical documents 1700–1911: printed pages, a newspaper, handwritten manuscripts and tables, each scored against an independent gold standard.

Bottom line. Tool choice depends on the document — and layout matters more than age. On clean text in a simple column (the 1911 booklet, even the worn 1700 broadside) all four tools are accurate and the choice is economic: olmOCR is fast, cheap and good enough. But on a dense four-column 1878 newspaper page olmOCR hallucinates — inventing place-names that were never printed — while Chandra 2, Gemini and Infinity Parser 2 stay accurate. For multi-column or complex layouts use a layout-aware tool (or human review); reserve the cheap tool for clean, simple pages.
